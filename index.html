Spanakopita

Spanakopita is a delicious traditional Greek dish common in tavernas across Europe. In rural Greece, smaller amounts of spinach are used, with the missing amount replaced with leeks, chard, and sorrel.

ingredients

  • 1 box phyllo dough
  • 1 lb butter
  • 6 eggs
  • 2 T breadcrumbs
  • 1 lb large curd cottage cheese
  • 1 lb feta cheese
  • 2 bunches scallions
  • 3 10 oz. packages frozen spinach
  • 2 onions
  • salt & pepper to taste

instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°.
  2. Thaw spinach in a colander, drain and squeeze out excess liquid.
  3. Chop onions and scallions, saute with a small amount of butter until soft.
  4. Add drained spinach, saute 5 more minutes, add salt and pepper.
  5. Beat eggs in large bowl, then add cottage cheese and crumbled feta cheese.
  6. Fold spinach and onion mixture into cheese and eggs.
  7. Add breadcrumbs, salt and pepper to mixture.
  8. Melt the butter and with the phyllo dough at room temperature, cover with
    a dish towel to prevent drying out.
  9. Lay one sheet of phyllo at a time in the 13 x 9 pan and brush with melted butter.
    Do this with about 10 sheets of phyllo.
  10. Spread a layer of the cheesy spinach across the pan.
  11. Alternate layers of several sheets of phyllo and cheesy spinach mixture.
  12. It's not important how you layer it. The cheesy spinach mixture could
    not be layered, but rather sandwiched between lots of layers of the phyllo and butter on top and bottom.
  13. Tent with foil and bake at 375° for 30 minutes, then reduce heat to 350° and bake for another 30 minutes.
    Take off foil tent for the last 15 minutes or until the top turns golden.

Scoring the phyllo dough with a sharp knife before baking may make cutting into portions easier once cooled. Opa!
